Video Matting / Segmentation

MatAnyone 2: Scaling Video Matting via a Learned Quality Evaluator

Top Paper (candidate). Uses a Matting Quality Evaluator (MQE) that needs no ground-truth alpha to classify each pixel as reliable/error, serving both as online training supervision and offline data arbiter. It fuses video matting (semantically stable) and image matting (boundary-friendly) to build VMReal, a real dataset with 28K clips / 2.4M frames, and adds a reference-frame strategy to handle appearance changes in long videos. Pure CNN, only the first-frame mask is needed, and it ranks first on all metrics across synthetic and real benchmarks.

Topic: Video Matting · Learned Quality Evaluator

Segmentation

VGGT-Segmentor: Geometry-Enhanced Cross-View Segmentation

Top Paper (candidate). Finds that VGGT's pixelwise point projection systematically drifts in ego–exo cross-view segmentation, while object-level attention remains stably aligned. Based on this, it freezes VGGT and adds only a lightweight Union Segmentation Head (Mask Prompt Fusion builds region consensus → Point-Guided Prediction demotes drifting points into sparse prompt → Mask Refinement iteratively cleans boundaries), then removes paired-label requirements through single-image self-supervised training. On Ego–Exo4D, 67.7/68.0 IoU sets SOTA, and the zero-paired-label pretrained version surpasses the previous fully supervised SOTA DOMR.

Topic: Cross-View Segmentation · Ego–Exo Correspondence

CodearXivBlog Digest

INSID3: Training-Free In-Context Segmentation with DINOv3

Top Paper (candidate). One annotated example is expected to segment any concept in any domain and at any granularity: this simultaneously needs cross-image semantic matching and in-image region grouping, so prior work either fine-tunes VFM (hurting generalization) or stitches DINOv2+SAM together (bulky and locked in granularity). INSID3 finds that a frozen DINOv3 already has both capabilities; the only blocker is a systematic positional bias (patches at the same coordinates in different images show spuriously high similarity regardless of semantics). It estimates a low-dimensional position subspace from noise images and removes it with training-free orthogonal projection; then raw features do agglomerative clustering (in-image grouping), debiased features do reverse correspondence + cross-image similarity seed selection, and finally cross-image semantics × in-image self-similarity multiplicative aggregation completes the mask. 9 benchmarks across four domains reach SOTA across the board, average +7.5% mIoU, only 304M (about 1/3 of the SAM pipeline), zero mask supervision; transferring debiasing to semantic correspondence adds another +6.6% PCK.

Topic: Training-Free In-Context Segmentation · DINOv3

ProjectCodeBlog Digest

Retrieve & Segment: Retrieve and Segment: Are a Few Examples Enough to Bridge the Supervision Gap in Open-Vocabulary Segmentation?

Top Paper (candidate). Open-vocabulary segmentation lags fully supervised methods because VLMs have only image-level supervision and language itself is ambiguous. RNS gives each class a few pixel-labeled images as visual support, turning zero-shot into few-shot; the core question becomes how to fuse text support and visual support. Unlike the late manual addition in kNN-CLIP / FREEDA, RNS first fuses the two modalities at the prototype level as f=λt+(1-λ)v, then uses retrieval (for each query patch, take k nearest neighbors from visual support) to assemble an on-the-fly training set for each test image and train a lightweight linear classifier per-image on A100 in <1 second. Three independent validations: Figure 5 retrieval ablation, Table 1 component removal, and Figures 3/4 full/partial support. DINOv3.txt+SAM with 20 images per class narrows the gap to full supervision to 11.5 mIoU, +34 over zero-shot, using only about 964 annotations (full supervision uses 20k).

Topic: Open-Vocabulary Segmentation · Retrieval-Augmented Test-Time Adaptation

CodearXivBlog Digest

SA-FARI: The SA-FARI Dataset: Segment Anything in Footage of Animals for Recognition and Identification

Top Paper (candidate). Multi-animal tracking (MAT) for wildlife is the foundation for species/individual/behavior recognition, but it has not taken off in ten years. The bottleneck is not the model but the dataset: existing datasets almost always sacrifice one of scale (<1h annotated), species diversity (≤5 species), spatiotemporal diversity (a few days in one protected area), or annotation quality (masks mostly auto-generated, no human verification). SA-FARI does all four mutually exclusive things at once: 741 independent sampling sites, 4 continents, 10 years (2014–2024), 99 species, 11,609 camera-trap videos, producing 16,224 frame-by-frame human-verified masklets and 942,702 boxes/masks (about 46 hours): 5× total duration and 2× species diversity over any previous MAT dataset, and the first large-scale wild MAT dataset with human-verified spatiotemporal segmentation. The key mechanism uses the SAM 3 data engine's human-AI-in-the-loop process (automatic pseudo-labeling→dual annotators + SAM2 in-loop correction→exhaustiveness checks) to reduce exhaustive annotation cost. Two independent validations: feeding the training set to SOTA models raises HOTA by 20+ points, and fine-tuning SAM 3 gives a threefold boost (training value); slicing the test set by small-mask/challenging/multi/night exposes failure modes: small targets hurt detection, and occlusion/motion hurts association (diagnostic value).

Topic: Multi-Animal Tracking Dataset · Camera Trap Video Segmentation

3D Vision

MuM: Multi-View Masked Image Modeling for 3D Vision

Top Paper (candidate). Extends MAE's unified masking + pixel reconstruction unchanged to arbitrary multi-view images of the same scene: all views are masked 75%, a shared-weight ViT-L encoder encodes each frame independently, and a lightweight ViT-B decoder with cross-frame attention reconstructs jointly: symmetric, no reference frame, and n=1 automatically degenerates to MAE. Geometry is compressed into the encoder as a byproduct of 'doing multi-view reconstruction under high masking'. Using only about DINOv3 1/30-scale compute, MuM as a frozen feature encoder surpasses DINOv3 and CroCo v2 on almost all geometry tasks such as multi-view three-dimensional reconstruction, dense matching, and relative pose (semantic tasks still lag DINOv3).

Topic: Multi-View MIM · Geometric Self-Supervision

Object Tracking / Correspondence

V²-SAM: Marrying SAM2 with Multi-Prompt Experts for Cross-View Object Correspondence

Top Paper (candidate). SAM2 needs 'coordinates in the target view' to segment, but under cross-view settings that coordinate is exactly unknown. V²-SAM uses two complementary prompt generators to 'create' it: V²-Anchor uses DINOv3 geometric features for cross-view matching→foreground constraint→sparse hierarchy→centroid, unlocking cross-view coordinate prompts for SAM2 for the first time (entirely non-trainable); V²-Visual uses VPMatcher to map appearance through both feature and structure paths; then three experts, Anchor/Visual/Fusion, plus the point-level cycle-consistency selector PCCS choose the most reliable prediction per instance. Ego-Exo4D 48.0 total IoU, DAVIS-17 78.8 J&F, and HANDAL-X 77.2 IoU are SOTA on all three leaderboards, with only 15M trainable parameters (about 1% of ObjectRelator).

Topic: Cross-View Object Correspondence · Ego–Exo

Object Tracking / Point Tracking

RWPT (Verifier-Guided): Real-World Point Tracking with Verifier-Guided Pseudo-Labeling

Top Paper (candidate). Long-horizon point tracking is almost entirely trained on synthetic data, and performance drops on real videos due to the sim-to-real domain gap. Self-training with pseudo-labels is the way out, but a single teacher's reliability fluctuates frame by frame, different teachers' errors are weakly correlated, and naive random selection/averaging amplifies noise into drift. This work redefines the problem as framewise reliability estimation and proposes verifier, a meta-model that does not directly predict coordinates: it uses frozen CoTracker3 features to compare the query's local appearance with each candidate's local appearance; through Candidate Transformer (restricted cross-attn + temporal self-attn), it outputs a framewise candidate reliability distribution, trained with a soft contrastive target from the negative softmax of candidate-to-GT distances, requiring only synthetic labels throughout. verifier can serve both as inference ensembling and as guidance for real-world fine-tuning. Base Track-On2 → Track-On-R: EgoPoints, RoboTAP, Kinetics, and DAVIS all reach SOTA (EgoPoints δ_avg 61.7→67.3), while using less data.

Topic: Point Tracking · Verifier-Guided Pseudo-Labeling

3D Vision / Visual Localization

AsymLoc: Towards Asymmetric Feature Matching for Efficient Visual Localization

Top Paper (candidate). The compute budgets at the two ends of visual localization are inherently asymmetric: map-library images are built offline and can freely use large models, while query images must be processed in real time on edge devices such as smart glasses. AsymLoc therefore proposes asymmetric visual localization: the map side uses a large teacher T to extract features offline, the query side uses a student S as small as 0.04M parameters to extract features online, and zero-parameter mutual nearest-neighbor matching aligns the two sides. The challenge is that features from two different models are naturally incompatible, and adding a 13M rematcher such as LightGlue is undesirable. AsymLoc puts alignment into the joint detector-descriptor space: geometric matching loss L_match anchors spatial correspondences via known homographies, and joint detector-descriptor distillation loss L_KD uses row/column bidirectional KL to make the student replicate the teacher's joint matchability distribution (aligning relations, not vectors). Under a SiLK teacher, a 0.13M student reaches 95.5% of teacher accuracy on Aachen with an order of magnitude less inference compute; a 25× smaller student still preserves 96%+ teacher accuracy, beating symmetric baselines and asymmetric distillation methods adapted from image retrieval across the board.

Topic: Visual Localization · Asymmetric Feature Matching

Pose Estimation / Motion Capture

MAMMA: Markerless Accurate Multi-person Motion Acquisition

Top Paper (candidate). Marker-based systems such as Vicon are the gold standard for motion capture, but they require reflective markers and manual segment-by-segment cleanup of noisy markers, and they especially fail when multiple people are in close contact. MAMMA upgrades supervision from sparse keypoints to 512 dense 2D surface landmarks: ViT + mask-CNN + a transformer decoder with one learnable query per landmark (MammaNet) outputs landmark position/uncertainty/visibility/contact for every frame and view; then SAM2 mask conditioning + symmetric epipolar distance performs 100% cross-view person association, and finally multi-view reprojection fits SMPL-X without pose priors or initialization. It is trained entirely on synthetic data MammaSyn (BEDLAM expanded to 32 cameras + interaction/hands). Hi4D 19-joint MPJPE 12.44mm (previous SOTA AvatarPose 32.10), held-out marker error against Vicon+MoSh is only 0.862mm, animation is visually indistinguishable, and the full capture-to-SMPL-X pipeline drops from about 72 hours to 26 hours.

Topic: Markerless Multi-Person Motion Capture · SMPL-X

Semantic Correspondence

MARCO: Navigating the Unseen Space of Semantic Correspondence

Top Paper (candidate). The mainstream semantic correspondence recipe is a DINOv2+Stable Diffusion dual encoder, accurate but close to 10 hundred-million parameters, and a model trained with sparse keypoints (≤20 points per image) collapses when switched to unseen keypoints/categories (Fig.2b: supervision shrinks the dense matchable flow across the image down around annotated points). MARCO uses only a single DINOv2 backbone (+ adapter + upsampling head with <5% parameters) and fills this gap with a training framework: first, coarse-to-fine Gaussian RBF distribution matching, with cosine annealing shrinking kernel width σ from 3 to 1, first aligning regions and then enforcing subpatch-level precision, avoiding soft-argmax L2 being pulled to the mean under multimodality; second, dense self-distillation via flow anchoring: an EMA teacher mines mutual-nearest-neighbor seeds, Delaunay triangulation + piecewise-affine warp densify them into a dense displacement field, then clusters in displacement space and uses GT keypoint anchor filtering for symmetry/occlusion mismatches, turning a handful of keypoints into thousands of reliable pseudo-correspondences so sparse supervision covers the whole object surface. SPair-71k/AP-10K/PF-PASCAL reach SOTA across the board, strictest threshold PCK@0.01 +8.9, unseen-keypoint SPair-U +5.1, unseen-category MP-100 +4.7, while being 3× smaller and 10× faster than diffusion dual encoders. Ablation ladder (SPair-U @0.10): no dense loss 41.8→MNN 52.5→+Delaunay 64.7→+GT anchor 67.5.

Topic: Semantic Correspondence · Single DINOv2 Backbone + Densified Self-Distillation

Virtual Colonoscopy / CTC

ColonFlatten: Virtual colon flattening method based on colonic outer surface

CMPB 2014. Switches the flattening distortion-correction benchmark from the rough inner colon surface to the smooth outer surface: uses the 'in-sphere range' of the geodesic distance field to cut colon-wall adhesions (OSE), and uses 'isodistant-surface velocity' to automatically split turns (DCA). On 60 CT cases, detection rate is 79.6% vs 67.1/71.9/72.7%, with false positives per case 0.16 vs 0.32/0.21/0.26.

Topic: Virtual Colonoscopy · Colon Flattening / Distortion Correction

PDFBlog Digest

CAD-CTC: High-Performance CAD-CTC Scheme Using Shape Index, Multiscale Enhancement Filters, and Radiomic Features

IEEE TBME 2016. Moves Shape Index and dot/line/plane multiscale enhancement filters onto a 'Gaussian-smoothed geodesic distance field (GDF)' to generate polyp candidates, then uses 640-dimensional radiomics + Random Forests to reduce false positives. ≥5mm reaches 98.1% by-polyp / 95.3% per-scan @1.3 FP/case; changing the coordinate system reduces candidate FP from 104.5 to 58.8.

Topic: Virtual Colonoscopy · Polyp CAD / CTC

PDFBlog Digest

Virtual eversion and rotation of colon based on outer surface centerline

Medical Physics 2010. Turns the colon 'inside out' (virtual eversion): the sampling centerline is changed from the jagged inner-surface centerline to the smooth outer-surface centerline, and eversion performs a distance-preserving reflection of the outer surface along sampling lines, E=2R-I. Failed eversion regions drop from 6.8→1.9 per case (71% fewer), eversion computation from 983s→75s (92% less), and reading time is about 77% shorter than VC.

Topic: Virtual Colonoscopy · Colon Eversion / CTC
