Medical VLM / Multimodal Understanding

LLaDA-MedV: Exploring Large Language Diffusion Models for Biomedical Image Understanding

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). The first biomedical masked diffusion VLM: it iteratively fills in a fixed-length fully masked canvas, making length an input hyperparameter rather than EOS early stopping; it leads ARM in open-ended dialogue and achieves three consecutive SOTA results on closed-ended VQA (84.93/92.31/95.15).

Topic: Medical AI · Masked Diffusion Language VLM

CodearXivBlog Digest

OctoMed: Data Recipes for State-of-the-Art Multimodal Medical Reasoning

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). While others are busy inventing new losses, stacking RL, and using larger backbone, OctoMed bets everything on 'what data to feed': distilling samples with reasoning trajectories from strong teacher models (DeepSeek-R1 / GPT-4o), using rejection sampling to keep only correct trajectories, and scaling to 800 ten-thousand samples / 68 hundred-million token (currently the largest medical multimodal reasoning dataset). With supervised fine-tuning alone, it tunes a 7B student (Qwen2.5-VL) to cross-distribution open-source SOTA, surpasses its teacher GPT-4o in multimodal classification (67.29 vs 53.96), and overall beats the 4× larger MedGemma-27B. Three mechanisms: multiple correct trajectories per question (1→16) act as regularization, lifting the MedQA peak from 75.16 to 85.01 (more valuable than training extra epoch); CoT helps reasoning, while direct helps perceptual classification; mixing trajectories of different lengths yields 'question-difficulty-calibrated reasoning length' without explicit supervision: long answers for hard questions, short answers for easy ones.

Topic: Medical AI · Data Recipe for Medical Multimodal Reasoning: Pure SFT Distillation + Rejection-Sampled Multi-Trajectory Data

arXivCVFBlog Digest

MedMO: Grounding and Understanding Multimodal Large Language Model for Medical Images

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). An open-source medical multimodal large model post-trained from Qwen3-VL-8B and using only public data: it separates the data-economics conflict between 'understanding images' and 'boxing lesions' into four-stage progressive post-training: 18.5M low-resolution (768²) data first establishes cross-modal alignment, then resolution rises to 1280² to inject grounding, instruction tuning aligns the QA style, and GRPO reinforcement comes last. The RL stage introduces purely geometric, verifiable bounding-box rewards (diagonal-normalized L1 + GIoU + Hungarian matching + FP/FN penalties), polishing spatial precision without poisoning language. MedMO-8B-Next reaches VQA average 72.7% and grounding six-task average 56.8 (strongest baseline 17.2), and 40.5 IoU on DeepLesion against 0.00 for multiple baselines.

Topic: Medical AI · Medical Multimodal Large Model / Four-Stage Post-Training for Understanding + Grounding

Image Segmentation / Pixel-Level Reasoning

VoxTell: Free-Text Promptable Universal 3D Medical Image Segmentation

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). Changes text fusion from MaskFormer's 'single final dot product' to 'fusion at every decoding scale + deep supervision': trained on 62K+ CT/MRI/PET and 1087 concepts, on 11 unseen datasets it reaches zero-shot average Dice 70.85 (runner-up SAT 51.23), and 50.2 under full-sentence prompts from real radiology reports while SAT gets only 0.0.

Topic: Medical AI · Text-Prompted 3D Medical Segmentation / Multi-Scale Vision-Language Fusion

CodearXivBlog Digest

IBISAgent: Reinforcing Pixel-Level Visual Reasoning in MLLMs for Universal Biomedical Object Referring and Segmentation

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). Does not modify the MLLM architecture or introduce an implicit <SEG> token: it recasts biomedical segmentation as a multi-step Markov decision process. The model alternates between emitting <think> reasoning and <action> textual click coordinates (Target,±1,Coordinate2d), calls frozen MedSAM2 to turn clicks into masks, overlays them semi-transparently back onto the original image as observations, and iteratively refines one click at a time. Two-stage training: cold-start SFT(456K automatically simulated click trajectories) + GRPO(no KL) RL with five types of fine-grained rule rewards (format/answer/region click placement: positive points land on FN, negative points land on FP/progressive IoU monotonically non-decreasing/trajectory length). Base Qwen2.5-VL-7B, 16×A100. It leads across all three test sets in IoU (in-domain 85.58, out-of-domain 80.63, external-hospital 72.09, best opponent only 50.74); removing fine-grained rewards collapses IoU to 11.77; the cost is ~3× inference latency.

Topic: Medical AI · Pixel-Level Visual Reasoning Agent / Recasting Segmentation as a Multi-Step MDP

3D Foundation Models / Representation and Generation

SPECTRE: Scaling Self-Supervised and Cross-Modal Pretraining for Volumetric CT Transformers

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). The first pure-transformer volumetric CT foundation model: it splits otherwise unaffordable 3D global attention into 'local ViT 24-layer close inspection inside windows (cost linear in the number of windows) + global ViT 4-layer full attention over window descriptors (cheap because G≪m)', with anisotropic patch / 3D RoPE, then performs DINOv3-style self-supervision followed by SigLIP alignment to radiology reports. Using only public CT data, 229,619 sequences, across 6 biomarker benchmarks it ranks first in 4/6 frozen-kNN tests and reaches CT-RATE zero-shot retrieval Recall@5 17.5 (CT-CLIP only 2.9).

Topic: Medical AI · Volumetric CT Foundation Model / Two-Level 3D Transformer + Vision-Language Alignment

CodearXivBlog Digest

BTB3D: Better Tokens for Better 3D: Advancing Vision-Language Modeling in 3D Medical Imaging

Medical AI (NeurIPS 2025). Repositions the bottleneck of 3D medical VLM from 'language backbone size' to 'fidelity and scalability of voxel token': 3D Haar wavelet frequency-domain compression + causal 3D convolution + lookup-free quantization + three-stage curriculum generalize to >300 slices without increasing memory, with report generation clinical F1 +40% and text-to-CT FID −75%.

Topic: Medical AI · 3D Medical Imaging VLM Tokenizer / Wavelet Frequency Domain + Causal Convolution + LFQ + Three-Stage Curriculum

Evaluation Benchmark

X-PCR: A Benchmark for Cross-modality Progressive Clinical Reasoning in Ophthalmic Diagnosis

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). Turns ophthalmic diagnosis from 'a pile of independent multiple-choice questions' into 'a six-stage reasoning chain with causal dependencies (IQA→AL→LC→DD→SG→CD) + cross-six-modality alignment reasoning', then adds difficulty stratification and uncertainty-aware (UAS/ECE) scoring: 26,415 expert-verified images, 177,868 VQA, 52 diseases, 51 public datasets. A single Chain Completion Rate exposes the flaw: among 21 MLLMs, the strongest, GPT-5, averages 76.24% on single steps, but only 24.47% across all six steps, and no model exceeds attending physicians (79.91%).

Topic: Medical AI · Ophthalmic Diagnostic Reasoning Benchmark / Six-Stage Dependent Reasoning Chain + Cross-Modal Alignment Evaluation

CodearXivBlog Digest

Med-CMR: A Fine-Grained Benchmark Integrating Visual Evidence and Clinical Logic for Medical Complex Multimodal Reasoning

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). The first medical multimodal benchmark to decompose clinical complex reasoning into fine-grained (seven-dimensional) capabilities: it splits the single overall score of 'medical multimodal reasoning' into 3 visual dimensions (small-object detection/detail discrimination/spatial understanding) + 4 reasoning dimensions (temporal prediction/causal reasoning/long-tail generalization/multi-source integration), with a dual-track protocol of MCQ accuracy + open-ended four-dimensional weighted LLM scoring (visual accuracy/ground-truth correctness each weight 4, consistency/coherence each weight 1). 20,653 questions come from real journal cases, covering 11 systems × 12 modalities, reviewed in two stages by experts + models. 18 models evaluated: GPT-5 ranks first (MCQ 57.81/open 48.70), but medically fine-tuned models are not consistently better than general models, and long-tail generalization is the hardest across the field.

Topic: Medical AI · Complex Medical Multimodal Reasoning Benchmark / Seven-Dimensional Capability Decomposition + Dual-Track Scoring

Surgery / Endoscopy

SurgCoT: Advancing Spatiotemporal Reasoning in Surgical Videos through a Chain-of-Thought Benchmark

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). The first cross-specialty surgical video CoT reasoning benchmark: it decomposes large spatiotemporal problems into three stages, 'video-level → clip-level → frame-level' (Q1→Q2→Q3, the higher-level Answer conditions the next level), annotates each level with a five-tuple Question→Option→Knowledge→Clue→Answer, and makes reason-then-decide explicit. 7 specialties, 35 procedures, 2,841 videos, 19,345 main questions + 59,177 subquestions, evaluating 10 MLLMs × 5 dimensions × three settings (BL/KE/FC). The sharpest finding: main questions are answered correctly while substeps are broadly wrong (GPT-5 main 87.58% but Q3 only 50.51%, Claude main 87.54% / Q3 30.44%), so high accuracy is often shortcutting rather than real reasoning.

Topic: Medical AI · Surgical Video Spatiotemporal Reasoning / Chain-of-Thought Benchmark

CodearXivBlog Digest

LEMON: A Large Endoscopic MONocular Dataset and Foundation Model for Perception in Surgical Settings

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). Private surgical data is not enough to feed foundation models, so LEMON switches to a fully public route: crawl about 1.8 ten-thousand videos from YouTube, then use a multi-level curation pipeline (video→frame→region, progressively removing non-surgical content by granularity) to reduce them to 4194 clips/938h/85M frames/35 procedures, making it the largest open surgical dataset to date. The foundation model LemonFM adds an 'augmented distillation' branch on top of DINO self-distillation: nearest-neighbor frames from other people undergoing the same procedure + temporally adjacent frames are also treated as positive views, explicitly training cross-patient/cross-frame invariance (3× distance threshold as a quality knob). Under both linear probing and full fine-tuning protocols, it leads across 4 tasks and 6 datasets: phase Jaccard up to +9.5pp, segmentation +10.3pp mDice, and fine-tuning with 50% labels still beats others using 100%.

Topic: Medical AI · Large Surgical Endoscopy Dataset + Augmented Self-Distillation Foundation Model

Pathology / Tissue

Uni-Hema: Unified Model for Digital Hematopathology

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). The first unified vision-language model for digital hematopathology (DHP): a shared backbone (ResNet-50 + DINO-DETR encoder-decoder + T5 text encoder-decoder), with Hema-Former routing 'hierarchical visual features + task-related query' at different granularities: CMF cross-modal fusion (language), TGVR top-K text modulation (detection/morphology), SCFE text-independent single-cell features (classification), and QGMF using Einstein summation to turn lesion query ⊗ pixel features into segmentation logits (pixels). 46 public sets (≈70 ten-thousand images + 22K QA + 7K MLM), single RTX 4090 for about 8 days, one 6-stage progressively frozen joint training run. Detection mean mAP50 61.1, classification mean F1 92.5, morphology 77.2 (AttriDet 62.6), and unseen-domain classification mean F1 90.8 ranks first; the cost is that small-sample tasks are overtaken by single-task models and segmentation edges are slightly blurred.

Topic: Medical AI · Digital Hematopathology / Unified Multi-Task Multimodal Model

CodearXivBlog Digest

CARE: A Molecular-Guided Foundation Model with Adaptive Region Modeling for Whole Slide Image Analysis

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). Changes WSI 'how to cut regions' from a fixed grid to learnable soft routing: soft inclusion assignments + top-3 candidates + 'semantic×spatial' score-based reassignment produce morphologically coherent adaptive regions (like word-level token in NLP). Region Structuring Loss pulls the 'expected ranking' toward E*=0.5 to resist collapse, SPF pools WSI/ROI representations with 'coverage prior ⊕ gated semantics', and a molecular-guided curriculum follows iBOT self-supervision→RNA→protein InfoNCE. With only about 1/10 data, 34,277 WSI, it leads on 33-task average molecular prediction LR 69.5 and survival C-index 58.0, while matching the strongest TITAN on morphology classification.

Topic: Medical AI · Pathology Whole-Slide Foundation Model / Adaptive Regions + Molecular-Guided Alignment

Report Generation

OraPO: Oracle-educated Reinforcement Learning for Data-efficient and Factual Radiology Report Generation

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). The first RL algorithm to couple DPO into GRPO: at the start of RRG training, about 30% of GRPO groups have all-0 rewards, advantage≈0, and nearly vanished gradients; OraPO treats these 'failed rollout' samples as ready-made negatives and ground-truth as positives for lightweight DPO, exactly supplying the gradient GRPO lacks. It adaptively anneals between GRPO/DPO by per-prompt Zero-Reward Rate (a 'teach first, then explore' flywheel), with recall-biased FactScore fact-level dense rewards. Single-stage pure RL, 3B Qwen2.5-VL, 1K samples, 4×A10, CheXpert Plus F1 0.341 / Recall 0.832, MIMIC-CXR F1 0.357, using 2–3 orders of magnitude less data than the previous SOTA.

Topic: Medical AI · Data-Efficient Radiology Report Generation / Oracle-Educated GRPO + Fact-Level Rewards

arXivBlog Digest

CURE: Curriculum-guided Multi-task Training for Reliable Anatomy Grounded Report Generation

Medical AI (CVPR 2026). Adds no new data and changes only the training methodology: it reconstructs finding-generation into region-by-region AGRG supervising both normal and abnormal findings (pulling the abnormal base rate back from phrase-grounding's ≈1 to the real value), then uses an error-normalized curriculum p_i=e_i/∑e_j to dynamically steer scarce sampling budget toward weak spots. A LoRA-tuned MedGemma-4B: anatomy-level localization IoU 0.249→0.601 (doubles), six-region abnormality hallucination 26.5%→8.78% (−18.6pt).

Topic: Medical AI · Reliable Radiology Report Generation / Error-Aware Curriculum + Anatomy-Level Report Reconstruction
