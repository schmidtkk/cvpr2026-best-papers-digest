4D-RGPT: can a video-language model learn depth and time without carrying a 4D expert at inference?

Paper2Html2026-06-09CVPR 2026 digest entry | Top Paper candidate | NVIDIASource: arXiv:2512.17012v4 PDF
Multimodal LLM4D UnderstandingPerceptual DistillationRegion-level VQAR4D-BenchMathJax checked

The short version. 4D-RGPT asks whether a multimodal large language model (MLLM) can answer questions about a specified video region when the answer depends on depth and time, not only appearance. The paper's answer is to train a region-level video MLLM with Perceptual 4D Distillation (P4D), where a frozen 4D perception model teaches the MLLM both hidden 4D features and explicit depth, flow, motion, and camera-ray signals. Those teacher-facing heads exist only during training, so the deployed model has the same inference path as the NVILA-Lite backbone rather than an extra 4D module. The paper also introduces R4D-Bench, a region-prompted visual question answering (VQA) benchmark built from dynamic 4D VQA sources and verified with human-in-the-loop grounding. The cleanest result is a matched-backbone one: 4D-RGPT-8B beats NVILA-Lite-8B on all six non-region 3D/4D benchmarks — by +2.0 to +6.2 points on the five multiple-choice columns, and +13.9 on VSTI-Bench, which is scored on a different relative-accuracy metric — and by +4.3 points on R4D-Bench average accuracy. The caveat is just as important: on R4D-Bench, GPT-4o still has a slightly higher average score, 42.8 versus 42.2, and 4D-RGPT sits a hair below its own baseline on the false-positive column (40.2 vs 40.7), within the noise the paper never quantifies. The useful contribution is therefore narrower than "MLLMs understand 4D scenes now": the paper shows a training recipe for injecting low-level 4D perception into a video MLLM without paying the teacher-model cost at inference.

One-line contribution4D-RGPT adds training-only 4D perception heads to NVILA-Lite and uses a frozen L4P teacher to make the MLLM's hidden states carry depth, flow, motion, camera-ray, and timestamp information.
Key mechanismP4D uses two losses: Latent Distillation (LD) aligns internal 4D features, and Explicit Distillation (ED) aligns predicted low-level 4D signals. Timestamp Positional Encoding (TPE) gives each sampled frame an explicit time coordinate.
Core evidenceAt matched 8B backbone size, P4D lifts NVILA-Lite-8B from 33.8 to 37.6 on STI-Bench, from 46.5 to 52.7 on VLM4D-real, and from 37.9 to 42.2 on R4D-Bench average accuracy.
Reproduction boundaryThe paper discloses the base checkpoints, data sources, loss weights, batch size, learning rate, hardware family, epoch count, and wall-clock duration. It does not state the number of A100 GPUs per node, so GPU-hours cannot be computed from the PDF alone.

1. Motivation: why does region-level 4D VQA expose a blind spot?

A normal video VQA question can often be answered from appearance and rough temporal order. Region-level 4D VQA is less forgiving. If the prompt asks for the speed of region \(\langle R_1\rangle\), the model must keep the same object identity across frames, estimate its displacement in 3D, and divide by the right time interval.

The paper argues that many MLLMs do not fail because their language decoder is weak. They fail because their visual tokens were trained mainly to describe what is visible, not to store metric depth, optical flow, motion state, or elapsed time. A supervised fine-tuning (SFT) answer such as "away from the camera" gives only a final token target. It does not tell the hidden state what the depth field should have been.

Q: Why not attach a depth or 4D model at inference time?

Guide: An external module can produce useful signals, but it changes the deployed system and must run on every input video.

A: 4D-RGPT uses the external model as a teacher during training only. The teacher shapes the MLLM's hidden states through distillation losses. At inference time, the extra heads are removed, so the answer comes from the video MLLM itself.

Takeaway: The paper is about moving 4D perception into the representation, not about building a larger inference pipeline.

2. Contributions: what is new, and what should not be overclaimed?

The authors make three explicit contributions. First, they propose 4D-RGPT, a region-level MLLM that adds training-only 4D perception modules and TPE to a video-language backbone. Second, they propose P4D, a distillation framework that transfers latent and explicit 4D representations from a frozen L4P teacher. Third, they introduce R4D-Bench, a benchmark for depth-aware dynamic scenes with region prompts.

The larger design pattern is also clear. When a perception skill exists in a specialist model but is missing from an MLLM, the specialist can supervise the MLLM's hidden states during training. The paper supports that pattern for 4D video perception. It does not prove that every specialist signal, every downstream domain, or every larger backbone will behave the same way.

How to read the headline result

When this page says "improves," it means a matched-backbone comparison unless stated otherwise. The strongest claim is 4D-RGPT-8B versus NVILA-Lite-8B under the same reported benchmark setup. Cross-family comparisons to GPT-4o, GPT-5, Gemini, or RL-trained specialist models are useful context, but they are not flat wins for 4D-RGPT.

3. Data and benchmarks: what is trained, what is evaluated?

The training data mixture combines four sources. VSTI-Bench contributes about 1.2K unique videos and 130K question-answer pairs from ScanNet and ScanNet++; Wolf contributes about 5K NuScenes-domain videos and 15K generated QA pairs; RoboFAC contributes about 10K simulated robot-arm videos and 65K conversations; SAT contributes about 190K simulated images and 170K QA pairs. The teacher supervision comes from L4P, a 4D perception model that predicts depth, flow, motion, and camera rays.

R4D-Bench is the paper's new evaluation set. It starts from non-region 4D VQA examples, extracts object mentions, obtains masks from source annotations or from GroundingDINO plus SAM2, renders a Set-of-Marks image, asks Qwen2.5-VL-32B-Instruct to match question objects to marked regions, and then uses human annotators to verify or correct the grounding. The paper reports 1,517 region-prompted VQAs in total; it also reports 418 static VQAs and 1,098 dynamic VQAs, which sum to 1,516, so that one-count discrepancy is present in the source.

4. Pipeline: how does P4D move teacher perception into the student?

The pipeline has two paths through the same video. The frozen teacher, L4P, processes the video and produces expert 4D representations. The student, 4D-RGPT, processes the same frames through the MLLM backbone, then uses training-only heads to decode comparable latent features and explicit signals. The losses compare teacher and student outputs, and their gradients update the student backbone.

Q: If the 4D heads are removed, where did the 4D skill go?

A: It has to live in the hidden states. The heads are only probes that make the hidden states match a teacher. If the backbone does not change, removing the probes would remove the skill too.

Takeaway: The training-design ablation is not a side detail; it tests whether gradients reached the backbone that remains at inference.

5. Method: latent features, explicit signals, and timestamps

The teacher model first defines what "4D perception" means. Given video \(V=[I^{(n)}]_{n=1:N}\), its 4D encoder \(E_{4D}\) produces a latent tensor, and each decoder \(D_m\) maps that tensor to a low-level modality \(m\). In this paper, \(\mathcal{M}\) includes depth, optical flow, motion, and camray. A camray is a per-pixel Plucker ray representation of camera geometry.

$$F_{4D}=E_{4D}(V)\in\mathbb{R}^{N'\times h'\times w'\times c'},\qquad P_m=D_m(F_{4D})$$
Eq. 1-2: the frozen teacher's latent 4D features and modality-specific 4D signals.

The student must learn comparable signals from its own hidden states. Each frame first passes through the vision encoder \(E_V\), the multimodal projector \(E_P\), and the language model. The hidden state \(F_{\mathrm{hidden}}^{(n)}\) is rearranged to match the teacher's spatiotemporal layout, then decoded by the training-only 4D perception decoder.

$$\hat F_{4D}^{(n')}=D_{\mathrm{4DP}}\!\left(\mathrm{Rearrange}\!\left(F_{\mathrm{hidden}}^{(n)}\right)\right),\qquad \hat P^m=D_m(\hat F_{4D})$$
Eq. 3-4: the student decodes latent and explicit 4D predictions from MLLM hidden states.

TPE handles the simplest missing variable: time. If frame \(I^{(n)}\) was sampled at time \(t^{(n)}\), the model adds sinusoidal coordinates to visual features before projection. The supplement states \(T=10{,}000\).

$$p^{(n)}[2i]=\sin\!\left(\frac{t^{(n)}}{T^{2i/D}}\right),\qquad p^{(n)}[2i+1]=\cos\!\left(\frac{t^{(n)}}{T^{2i/D}}\right)$$
Eq. 5: Timestamp Positional Encoding for even and odd feature dimensions.

The two distillation losses ask the same question at two resolutions. LD compares hidden 4D feature tensors. ED compares interpretable 4D outputs.

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{LD}}=\sum_{n'=1}^{N'}\Delta_{\mathrm{LD}}\!\left(F_{4D}^{(n')},\hat F_{4D}^{(n')}\right)$$
Eq. 6: Latent Distillation aligns teacher and student 4D features.
$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ED}}=\sum_{n=1}^{N}\sum_{m\in\mathcal{M}}\lambda_m\,\Delta_m\!\left(P_m^{(n)},\hat P_m^{(n)}\right)$$
Eq. 7: Explicit Distillation aligns modality-level signals, weighted by \(\lambda_m\).

The final training objective adds SFT to the two P4D losses. The supplement specifies Smooth-L1 distance for \(\Delta_{\mathrm{LD}}\) and every \(\Delta_m\), with \(\lambda_{\mathrm{depth}}=1.0\), \(\lambda_{\mathrm{flow}}=0.1\), \(\lambda_{\mathrm{motion}}=0.05\), and \(\lambda_{\mathrm{camray}}=0.05\). It also specifies \(\alpha=0.5\) and \(\beta=0.1\) for the weighted total loss.

$$\mathcal{L}=\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SFT}}+\alpha\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{LD}}+\beta\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ED}}$$
Eq. A8: total loss disclosed in the supplement.

Q: Why does the method need both LD and ED?

A: LD teaches the shape of the teacher's internal 4D representation. ED teaches measurable outputs such as depth and flow. LD without ED can be abstract but hard to inspect; ED without LD can miss the teacher's richer structure.

Takeaway: The method is strongest when the student matches both the teacher's hidden geometry and its explicit predictions.

6. Training and compute: what is disclosed?

The paper's main experiments use NVILA-Lite-8B as the backbone. NVILA-Lite uses SigLIP as the vision tower and Qwen2 as the language model. The supplement also evaluates a 15B variant, but the headline architecture and most ablations use the 8B model.

7. Experiments: what improves, and against which baseline?

The main non-region benchmark result is a matched-backbone improvement over NVILA-Lite-8B. The 8B model gains on all six columns, with the largest gain on VSTI-Bench. Against proprietary models, the result is mixed: Gemini-2.5-Pro is higher on STI and VLM4D-real, GPT-5 is higher on MMSI and OmniSpatial, and Gemini-1.5-Pro is 0.1 point higher on SAT; the paper reports no uncertainty, so the 0.1 point SAT gap should not be overread.

Q: Do the ablations prove P4D, rather than just more 4D data, caused the gain?

A: They make that case with matched ingredients. 4D-SFT uses the data but reaches 40.1 R4D average. P4D reaches 42.2 and does not require the teacher feature at inference. The modality table then shows that the best row uses both latent and explicit distillation.

Takeaway: The evidence points to feature-level distillation plus trainable student representations, not merely to exposure to more QA examples.

Q: What is the most likely overstatement?

A: "The model understands 4D scenes." The safer statement is: with P4D and TPE, an NVILA-Lite video MLLM becomes better at the paper's depth- and time-sensitive VQA benchmarks, while still struggling with exact numerical reasoning and some task columns.

Takeaway: Treat 4D-RGPT as a strong representation-training result, not as a finished 4D reasoning system.

10. Summary: the mental model to keep

In one sentence: 4D-RGPT uses a frozen 4D teacher as a training-time measuring device, forcing a video MLLM's hidden states to predict depth, flow, motion, camera rays, and time-aware 4D features before removing the measuring device at inference.

Three takeaways

  1. P4D changes the representation, not the inference graph. The teacher and 4D heads supervise training but are not carried into deployment.
  2. The main result is matched-backbone. 4D-RGPT-8B is much stronger than NVILA-Lite-8B on the reported 3D/4D benchmarks and R4D-Bench.
  3. The evidence is scoped. The model is not best in every table column, and the supplement names precise numerical estimation as a remaining weakness.

A good reading path through the paper is: Figure 1 for the task, Figure 2 plus Eq. 6-7 for the distillation mechanism, Table 4-5 for why P4D is not just SFT, and Table 3 for the region-level evaluation boundary.
